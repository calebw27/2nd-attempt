Kung Pao Chicken

This recipe is as authentic as it gets. Don't let the steps intimidate you. Get the prep out of the way and this dish is one you will never stop cooking.

Ingredients Needed:

For the Marinade:
  • 12 oz. chicken breast, cut into 3/4 inch cubes
  • 1 teaspoon oil
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon shaoxing wine
  • A pinch of ground white pepper

Combine all the ingredients in a bowl and set aside to marinate for 20 minutes.

For the Sauce:
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 3 tablespoons water
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch

Mix together all the ingredients in a medium bowl and set aside.

Organize the rest of your ingredients:
  • 3 tablespoons oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, smashed and sliced
  • 2 thin slices ginger, minced
  • 2 dried red chilies, deseeded and chopped (these can be quite spicy, so adjust according to your own tastes)
  • 1/2 teaspoon Sichuan peppercorns
  • 6 scallions, white portions only, cut into 3/4 inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup of roasted peanuts

Time to Start Cooking

Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a wok over high heat. Sear the chicken, remove from the wok to a bowl, and set aside.

Turn the heat to low and add another tablespoon oil. Add the garlic, ginger, chilies, and Sichuan peppercorns. Cook for a minute or two until fragrant.

Add the chicken back to the pan, along with the scallions. Turn up the heat to high and stir-fry for a minute. Then use your finger to stir up your prepared sauce (the cornstarch will have settled to the bottom, so make sure it is well incorporated). Add the sauce to the wok and stir-fry for another minute. The sauce should thicken very quickly.

Finally, add the peanuts. Give everything a final stir and serve.

To quote my favorite television character, "George likes his chicken spicy."
